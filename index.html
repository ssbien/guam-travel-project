Why Guam?

Guam, Where America's Day Begins

Guam is the part of the U.S.A. you may never have heard of. That's because this little 30-mile-long speck of an island in Micronesia is marooned in a wide swath of the Pacific Ocean—Tokyo and Manila, two of the largest nearby cities, are each still some 1,600 miles north and west, respectively. But that situation within reach of much of eastern Asia is exactly why the United States made Spain hand it over as a territory in 1898. Over several subsequent wars, this tropical island has proved to be a useful military base. Its rich local history stretches back centuries, but today, Guam's personality is a distinct mix of pan-Pacific and all-American.

Guam lies on the other side of the International Date Line, 13 hours ahead of Washington, D.C. That means it's the first American holding to enter every new day. The tourism department point out that the island is "Where America's Day Begins." It's also a place where Asian people begin their own American experiences, whether it's in the '50s-style diners packed with Japanese and Korean tourists, the cluster of shopping malls in the main town of Hagatna, or the t-shirt shops laden with Old Glory.

Restaurants

Places To Eat On Guam

Capricciosa Ristorante Italiano

A fun and casual restaurant that is popular as a place where large portions of authentic Italian dishes could be shared and enjoyed in a relaxing atmosphere.

Address:

626 Pale San Vitores Rd, Tumon

What I like about it

This is a great restaurant to eat at with a large group of people and a lot of Italian food options. Always has something on the menu for every customer!

Chamorro Village

The Wednesday Night Market here is full of both local and international cuisines that attract island residents and visitors. Chamorro Village is the perfect place for visitors to experience being part of the island’s extended family.

Address:

Paseo Loop, Hagatna

What I like about it

A great hub for a wide variety of local and international food, local businesses, and Guam culture. You can find local food, products, and crafts.

Learn more

Jamaican Grill

Specializing in all things BBQ, they are a local favorite for their ribs, chicken, and rice dishes. They also offer up savory steak, seafood, and juicy burgers fresh off the grill! Their Jamaican Jerk spices are imported directly from Jamaica!

Address:

165 Skyline Drive, Suite 800, Tamuning

What I like about it

Great vibes with good food. Perfect for catering, lunch plates, or for a family lunch/dinner after spending the day at the beach.
